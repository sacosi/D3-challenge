Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

This chart shows the correlation between health risks and factors such as proverty, age and income for each of the 50 states in the United States of America

Starting with poverty, it is very clear from the chart above that the states where the population is poorer, the number of people without healthcare is higher. With some States as exceptions, the same can be confirmed with the obesity rate and the people that smoke. As easily predicted, but confirmed by this data,the states with higher levels of poverty result in higher obesity and smoking rates.

When looking at how the median age impacts a state on health risks, it's not so linear as the poverty. However, we can see that the younger the population in a state is the more they lack healthcare, which could be explained by the struglle a lot of young employees have to find initial jobs with helthcare insurance. Even though older population have better healthcare, the data shows that they smoke more. So summarizing, states with older population have more smokers but also more people with healthcare coverage. The obisity rate doesn't seem to be impacted by the age.

Last but not least, the income seems to impact significantly the 3 measures the same way. Basically, the lower the median household income is for a state the higher values it has of smokers, obesity and people without healthcare.
